Tyler Farrell Poetry

Tyler Farrell is an English professor at Marquette University and a published poet. Tyler hired me to build him a personal website to showcase his work:

He wanted the website to be professional and clean, describing himself as not the kind of professor "with patched sleeves." Much of his work is inspired by Irish poetry, so I used his images of Ireland as the anchor elements. The site is responsive and displays a smaller photo (which loads faster) for mobile. Since there are other Tyler Farrells, I recommended targeting the word poetry to differentiate himself. The website is the second result on Google (behind his Marquette University profile).

"It has been a pleasure working with Alex and his hard work on my site has been very professional, and highly appreciated. Alex has made my website quite effective and always handled adjustments with grace and aplomb." - Tyler Farrell

The Juergens Family Foundation

The Juergens Family Foundation (JFF) was founded in 2012 by Sharon Juergens to create a family organization to seek out and fund projects around the world to make the world a better place. JFF mainly focuses on educational and health-related projects that are proven to be sustainable, but, in the end, is interested in any project that positively impacts mankind.

I serve as a Director, helping to research and decide which causes to fund, and I donate my time and expertise to designing and maintaining the Wordpress website.

The Burmese Immersion Project (BIP) works directly with refugees from Southeast Asia who reside in Milwaukee, WI. Committed to educational and social enhancement, the Burmese Immersion Project empowers refugees to navigate American society, access resources, and become community leaders.

Team Player

My skill in HTML, CSS, Javascript, jQuery, PHP and the Adobe Creative Cloud applications grew in leaps and bounds, and I continue to improve every day.

Making my teammates better

I always look for innovative ways to make save time by supporting my co-workers. I used the open-source CMS SiteCake to create a template for our weekly In The News email:

It allows the writers to edit the emails as if they were a word document, cutting the middleman (myself) from the process completely, freeing me to work on other projects. It has version control, automatically creates back-ups and best of all, outputs clean code.

In the spirit of empowering my co-workers, I taught myself how to use the YouTube API to automatically pull upload videos and their captions and thumbnails into a website. This allows our video producers to add videos directly to the site without having to use the web team, saving everyone time. Photos and more info will be available when the site goes live early this year.

Making a difference

I want to work for Marquette for the same reason I wanted to attend as an undergraduate student. I believe in Marquette's mission. If hired, I want to start a partnership between the Office of Marketing and Communication and Cristo Rey Jesuit High School. Cristo Rey is part of a nationwide network of 30 Catholic, college preparatory high schools for students from limited financial means. All Cristo Rey schools share a unique and innovative model which empowers students to pursue higher education through a rigorous curriculum and provides invaluable professional work experience through the Corporate Work Study Program.

I believe these students could be great employees, and this partnership would align with Marquette's four pillars and further its mission to help others in the name of God.
